Emotional acknowledgment before every intervention

Clinically, the correct sequence for ADHD support is: acknowledge → validate → act. Every existing app goes directly to advice. Attune's intervention agent should always begin with one sentence of recognition: "Getting stuck on something that felt important makes sense — that's not failure, that's your brain protecting you from a mismatch." Then it acts. This single structural change makes Attune feel fundamentally different from every tool on the market. It is also the thing ADHD users say they need most. Prompt constraint — no code.

It reframes Attune from reactive (responds to problems) to proactive (anticipates them). That distinction is exactly what judges mean by "agentic." Section 06 — The Perfected 4-Minute Demo Script

Every second is engineered for one thing: judge memory

The demo is not a product tour. It is a story. Protagonist: Alex, who has undiagnosed ADHD and has been losing hours every day to executive dysfunction. Antagonist: the moment when Alex gets stuck and every other tool fails. Resolution: Attune acts.

Structure it like a film, not a tutorial. Never narrate what judges can already see on screen. Every sentence should add context the UI cannot show.

The Two Rules of a Winning Demo

Rule 1: One working loop that completes flawlessly is worth more than three impressive features that each half-work. Rehearse the core loop (Screen 1 → Screen 2 → Intervention → Plan Restructure) until it is effortless.

Rule 2: End every major moment with a sentence about what it means, not what it does. "The agent just changed the plan" → "Alex never had to figure out what to do next. That's what executive function replacement looks like."
